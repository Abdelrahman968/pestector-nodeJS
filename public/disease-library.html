Disease Library

Explore a comprehensive library of plant diseases and healthy states with detailed information.

Apple

Apple Scab

Symptoms: Dark, velvety spots on leaves and fruit, often leading to defoliation.

Cause: Fungus Venturia inaequalis.

Management: Apply fungicides in early spring, remove fallen leaves to reduce fungal spores.

Black Rot

Symptoms: Dark lesions on fruit, cankers on branches, and leaf spots.

Cause: Fungus Botryosphaeria obtusa.

Management: Prune infected branches, apply fungicides during bloom.

Cedar Apple Rust

Symptoms: Yellow-orange spots on leaves, galls on cedar trees.

Cause: Fungus Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae.

Management: Remove nearby cedar trees, apply fungicides in spring.

Healthy

Symptoms: No visible signs of disease, vibrant green leaves, and healthy fruit.

Cause: N/A.

Management: Maintain proper watering, fertilization, and pruning.

Blueberry

Healthy

Symptoms: No disease signs, glossy green leaves, plump berries.

Cause: N/A.

Management: Ensure well-drained acidic soil, regular watering.

Cherry

Powdery Mildew

Symptoms: White powdery coating on leaves and stems, distorted growth.

Cause: Fungus Podosphaera clandestina.

Management: Improve air circulation, apply sulfur-based fungicides.

Healthy

Symptoms: No disease signs, shiny green leaves, firm cherries.

Cause: N/A.

Management: Prune regularly, avoid overwatering.

Corn

Cercospora Leaf Spot

Symptoms: Grayish-white spots with dark borders on leaves, reduced photosynthesis.

Cause: Fungus Cercospora zeae-maydis.

Management: Use resistant hybrids, apply fungicides during early growth.

Common Rust

Symptoms: Orange-brown pustules on leaves, reduced yield.

Cause: Fungus Puccinia sorghi.

Management: Plant resistant varieties, apply fungicides if needed.

Northern Leaf Blight

Symptoms: Large, grayish-green lesions on leaves, often cigar-shaped.

Cause: Fungus Exserohilum turcicum.

Management: Use resistant hybrids, rotate crops, apply fungicides.

Healthy

Symptoms: No disease signs, vibrant green leaves, well-formed ears.

Cause: N/A.

Management: Ensure proper fertilization, irrigation, and pest control.

Grape

Black Rot

Symptoms: Dark spots on leaves and fruit, fruit shrivels into mummies.

Cause: Fungus Guignardia bidwellii.

Management: Remove infected fruit, apply fungicides during early growth.

Esca (Black Measles)

Symptoms: Dark streaks in wood, leaf discoloration, sudden vine death.

Cause: Complex of fungi including Phaeomoniella chlamydospora.

Management: Prune during dry weather, avoid wounding vines.

Leaf Blight

Symptoms: Yellowing leaves with brown, necrotic spots, premature leaf drop.

Cause: Bacterium Xanthomonas campestris.

Management: Improve air circulation, apply copper-based bactericides.

Healthy

Symptoms: No disease signs, vibrant green leaves, plump grapes.

Cause: N/A.

Management: Prune annually, ensure proper trellising and watering.

Orange

Haunglongbing

Symptoms: Yellowing leaves, stunted growth, bitter fruit, tree decline.

Cause: Bacterium Candidatus Liberibacter asiaticus, spread by psyllids.

Management: Control psyllid populations, remove infected trees, use resistant rootstocks.

Peach

Bacterial Spot

Symptoms: Small, water-soaked spots on leaves and fruit, leading to leaf drop.

Cause: Bacterium Xanthomonas arboricola pv. pruni.

Management: Use resistant varieties, apply copper sprays, avoid overhead watering.

Healthy

Symptoms: No disease signs, lush green leaves, juicy peaches.

Cause: N/A.

Management: Prune annually, ensure good drainage, fertilize appropriately.

Pepper

Bacterial Spot

Symptoms: Dark, water-soaked spots on leaves and fruit, yellow halos.

Cause: Bacterium Xanthomonas campestris pv. vesicatoria.

Management: Use disease-free seeds, apply copper sprays, rotate crops.

Early blight

Symptoms: Dark, water-soaked spots on leaves and fruit, yellow halos.

Cause: Bacterium Xanthomonas campestris pv. vesicatoria.

Management: Use disease-free seeds, apply copper sprays, rotate crops.

Healthy

Symptoms: No disease signs, glossy green leaves, firm peppers.

Cause: N/A.

Management: Provide consistent watering, fertilize with balanced nutrients.

Potato

Early Blight

Symptoms: Concentric rings on leaves (target spots), yellowing foliage.

Cause: Fungus Alternaria solani.

Management: Rotate crops, apply fungicides, remove plant debris.

Late Blight

Symptoms: Dark, water-soaked spots on leaves, white fungal growth on undersides.

Cause: Oomycete Phytophthora infestans.

Management: Use resistant varieties, apply fungicides, destroy infected plants.

Healthy

Symptoms: No disease signs, green foliage, well-formed tubers.

Cause: N/A.

Management: Hill plants to protect tubers, maintain soil moisture.

Raspberry

Healthy

Symptoms: No disease signs, vibrant green leaves, juicy berries.

Cause: N/A.

Management: Prune canes after harvest, ensure good air circulation.

Soybean

Healthy

Symptoms: No disease signs, green leaves, well-formed pods.

Cause: N/A.

Management: Rotate crops, monitor for pests, maintain soil fertility.

Squash

Powdery Mildew

Symptoms: White powdery coating on leaves, reduced fruit quality.

Cause: Fungus Podosphaera xanthii.

Management: Improve air circulation, apply fungicides, avoid overhead watering.

Strawberry

Leaf Scorch

Symptoms: Dark purple or brown spots on leaves, leaf margins dry out.

Cause: Fungus Diplocarpon earlianum.

Management: Remove infected leaves, apply fungicides, improve air circulation.

Healthy

Symptoms: No disease signs, green leaves, sweet berries.

Cause: N/A.

Management: Use mulch, water at the base, fertilize regularly.

Tomato

Bacterial Spot

Symptoms: Small, water-soaked spots on leaves and fruit, yellow halos.

Cause: Bacterium Xanthomonas campestris pv. vesicatoria.

Management: Use disease-free seeds, apply copper sprays, rotate crops.

Early Blight

Symptoms: Concentric rings on leaves (target spots), yellowing foliage.

Cause: Fungus Alternaria solani.

Management: Rotate crops, apply fungicides, stake plants to improve air flow.

Late Blight

Symptoms: Dark, water-soaked spots on leaves, white fungal growth on undersides.

Cause: Oomycete Phytophthora infestans.

Management: Use resistant varieties, apply fungicides, avoid overhead watering.

Leaf Mold

Symptoms: Yellow spots on upper leaf surfaces, grayish mold on undersides.

Cause: Fungus Passalora fulva.

Management: Increase air circulation, apply fungicides, remove infected leaves.

Septoria Leaf Spot

Symptoms: Small, grayish-white spots with dark edges on leaves, leaf drop.

Cause: Fungus Septoria lycopersici.

Management: Remove infected leaves, apply fungicides, avoid overhead watering.

Spider Mites

Symptoms: Stippling on leaves, fine webbing, yellowing foliage.

Cause: Pests (Tetranychus urticae).

Management: Use insecticidal soap, introduce predatory mites, maintain humidity.

Target Spot

Symptoms: Concentric rings with a grayish center on leaves, leaf drop.

Cause: Fungus Corynespora cassiicola.

Management: Improve air circulation, apply fungicides, remove infected debris.

Yellow Leaf Curl Virus

Symptoms: Leaves curl upwards, turn yellow, stunted growth.

Cause: Virus (TYLCV), spread by whiteflies.

Management: Control whitefly populations, use resistant varieties, remove infected plants.

Mosaic Virus

Symptoms: Mottled yellow and green patterns on leaves, distorted growth.

Cause: Virus (Tomato mosaic virus), spread by contact.

Management: Use virus-free seeds, disinfect tools, remove infected plants.

Healthy

Symptoms: No disease signs, green leaves, firm tomatoes.

Cause: N/A.

Management: Stake plants, water consistently, fertilize with balanced nutrients.

Frequently Asked Questions

Plant diseases are commonly caused by fungi, bacteria, viruses, and environmental factors. Fungi like powdery mildew and rust thrive in humid conditions, while bacteria such as Xanthomonas cause spots and blights. Viruses, like the Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus, are often spread by insects. Poor cultural practices, such as overwatering or poor air circulation, can also contribute to disease development.

Preventing diseases involves good garden hygiene: rotate crops annually, use disease-resistant varieties, ensure proper spacing for air circulation, water at the base of plants, and remove plant debris. Regularly monitor for pests, as they can spread diseases, and apply organic or chemical treatments as needed. Healthy soil management, such as proper fertilization, also helps plants resist diseases.

If your plant is infected, first identify the disease using tools like Pestectors AI detection. Remove and destroy infected plant parts (do not compost them). Apply appropriate treatments, such as fungicides for fungal diseases or copper sprays for bacterial issues. Isolate the plant if possible to prevent spread, and improve cultural practices to reduce stress on the plant. For severe infections, especially viral ones, you may need to remove the entire plant.
